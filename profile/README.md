## 💡 Overview

<a href="#goProQuik">GoPro Quik</a> is the official GoPro media management and video editing application for Mac — providing the import, organization, editing, and sharing tools that GoPro camera users need to turn raw action footage into finished videos without a complex professional video editor.

The <a href="#goProQuik">automatic highlight reel creation</a> analyzes imported GoPro footage and assembles a highlight video automatically — identifying the most dynamic moments using HiLights markers, motion analysis, and audio cues, then cutting them together with music synchronization into a shareable video without manual editing. Users who want to share footage quickly without editing experience can produce a polished highlight video in minutes from a day's worth of GoPro recordings. The <a href="#goProQuik">GoPro camera connection and import</a> connects directly to GoPro cameras via USB or WiFi — importing footage, managing camera settings, updating firmware, and controlling the camera from the Mac desktop. SD card import handles footage from cameras not directly connected, with automatic duplicate detection to prevent re-importing footage already in the library.

The <a href="#goProQuik">media library organization</a> manages the GoPro footage library with date-based organization, manual collections, and search — finding specific footage by date, trip, or activity across a library that grows with every recording session. The <a href="#goProQuik">editing tools</a> provide manual control beyond the automatic highlight: trimming clips, adjusting playback speed for slow-motion and time-lapse effects, adding music from the built-in licensed music library, applying color adjustments, and adding text overlays for titles and captions.</p>

<a href="#goProQuik">Speed ramp effects</a> apply the slow-motion and speed-up transitions that action video editing uses to emphasize key moments — slowing down at the peak of a jump, speeding up through the setup, and returning to normal speed for the landing in a single smooth speed transition. <a href="#goProQuik">Music synchronization</a> aligns edit cuts to the beat of the selected music track — automatic beat detection matches the visual rhythm of the edit to the musical rhythm for the synchronized feeling that makes action highlight reels compelling.

<a href="#goProQuik">Direct social sharing</a> exports finished videos directly to Instagram, Facebook, YouTube, and other platforms — sharing the finished video without downloading and re-uploading through a separate application. <a href="#goProQuik">GoPro subscription cloud backup</a> automatically uploads GoPro footage to cloud storage through the GoPro subscription service — maintaining a remote backup of all footage without manual management.</p>

## 🙋 FAQ

<details>
<summary>Does GoPro Quik connect to GoPro cameras?</summary>

Yes. Quik connects to GoPro cameras via USB and WiFi for footage import, firmware updates, and settings management.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does Quik create videos automatically?</summary>

Yes. The automatic highlight reel feature analyzes footage and creates a highlight video with music synchronization without manual editing.

</details>

<details>
<summary>What editing tools does Quik provide?</summary>

Clip trimming, speed ramp effects, color adjustments, music selection, text overlays, and manual arrangement of clips alongside the automatic highlight tools.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does Quik support 360 video from GoPro MAX?</summary>

Yes. 360-degree video from GoPro MAX and other 360 cameras is supported for viewing, reframing, and export.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Is GoPro Quik free?</summary>

Basic features are available free. Full features including cloud backup and unlimited highlights require a GoPro subscription.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can Quik share directly to social media?</summary>

Yes. Direct export to Instagram, YouTube, Facebook, and other platforms is available from within Quik.

</details>

<details>
<summary>What are HiLights markers?</summary>

HiLights are tags placed on the GoPro camera during recording to mark key moments. These carry through to Quik for use in highlight selection.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does Quik run on Apple Silicon?</summary>

Yes. Current versions support Apple Silicon Macs natively.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does Quik sync between Mac and iPhone?</summary>

Yes. GoPro subscription connects the Mac and mobile Quik applications for cross-device access to projects and media.

</details>
